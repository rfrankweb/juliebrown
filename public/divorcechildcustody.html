Divorce and Child Custody

Contested Divorce

When neither spouse can agree on one or more settlements during a divorce, they are dealing with a contested divorce. In today’s age, these are more common. These often have to be settled in a court of law, and you must have a great attorney on your side that settles disputes fairly and in your favor.

Parenting Agreements

If you have children while going through a divorce, a parenting agreement must be formed. This is a document that states things such as where the child will be going, who is responsible for the child, etc. This is essential for providing stability to the child and preventing them from wondering where they should be at a certain point. An attorney can help write the document and make sure that everything is laid out and that nothing is missing.

Support and Support Modification

Because it may be hard to keep up on payments as a single parent with one or more children, child support is an option. Child support is paid for by at least one of the spouses, usually the one without custody. These can help keep your child off of welfare and can cover many expenses from food to entertainment. A divorce attorney can help set this up for your family.
Child support payments are based on many factors, such as housing or employment. But things change. One or more of the spouses may lose their job or be unable to make payments. If this occurs, an attorney can help modify child support can be modified depending on what is going on in the parents’ lives.

Property Division

When going through a divorce, all assets and debts need to be divided across the spouses evenly. It can be confusing when finding out who gets what, and many factors are involved. These issues can occasionally be solved by the spouses, but during a divorce, many things are going through your mind, and your judgment may be clouded. An attorney must help divide all of your assets and debts evenly and fairly.

Community Property

Community property is a law that is recognized in eight states, California being one of them. Essentially, community property is anything bought during the marriage, such as shared income or housing. The second kind of property is separate property and is anything owned before the marriage. The community property law states that in a court of law, this property is split across the spouses equally, and is then treated as separate property. An attorney is important in making sure property division is fair and just.

Pension and Retirement Benefits

Under divorce law, any form of pension given to the family during a marriage is considered an asset to both spouses and can be split evenly. Although this is true, this is not done automatically and must be requested. A divorce attorney can help you through the process so that the court can award it to you.

Allocation of Debts

Many families create debt during the marriage, whether it be home loans or emergencies. When going through a divorce, allocating debts is just as important as separating assets. To make sure that the distribution of debts is fair to the spouses, it is important to have a good attorney to work things out.

Pre and Post Nuptial Agreements

Prenuptial and postnuptial agreements are agreements on how finances will be divided up after a divorce. Prenups(prenuptial agreements) happen before the marriage and vice versa for postnups(postnuptial agreements). These are helpful for attorneys to more easily figure out the finances of a marriage, especially with tax laws. An attorney may create one if there is a lot of wealth involved in the marriage, if it is not one of the spouse’s first marriages, or if a death occurring to one or more of the spouses is likely in the moderately near future.

Settlement Agreements

There must be a written document discussing all of the factors of divorce. This is known as a settlement agreement. These make sure that there is a record of the divorce that can be used as a reference by the government or the spouses. An attorney will help write one out that covers every detail in the divorce.

Domestic Partnership/Same-Sex Marriage

When two individuals live together in the same home(known as a domestic partnership), it is required to certify that the partnership has ended. Ending a domestic partnership can be as easy as filling out a form and paying a fee but can get more complicated depending on factors like location, property, and child custody. A divorce attorney is helpful as domestic partnership laws differ from location to location, and ending the partnership can be as messy as a divorce.

Judgment Enforcement

When a judgment about a divorce is made by a court of law, it is up to the spouses and other members of the parties to make sure that these decisions are enforced. This can often be difficult for individuals. A divorce attorney can help to make sure that both parties are keeping up to what was agreed to during a divorce.
