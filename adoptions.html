Adoptions

Unplanned/Unwanted Pregnancy

If you find yourself with an unplanned/unwanted pregnancy and you are unable to care for a child, adoption is an option for the child. Here at Brown and Associates, we can help to set your child up for adoption as well as help to find a forever family.

Facilitate the Adoption Process

To legally adopt a child, it is required that an adoption attorney works through the paperwork and guides you through the process of adoption. Adoption is a very complicated procedure, and you must have a great adoption attorney that helps to remove the stress of adoption.

Home Study Counseling

During the adoption, a home study will be performed on your home. This is required by the state of California, and checks to see what the child’s home life will be like, and helps the adoption worker understand who you are as a family. This also educates the family on adoption and evaluates the family.

Stepparent Adoptions

If a spouse has had a child before the marriage, the other person can adopt the child into the family, known as a stepparent adoption. This creates feelings of permanence and recognizes the individual as a parent. These are similar to regular adoptions, and you must have an adoption attorney to guide you through the process.

Contested Adoptiions

A contested adoption occurs when one biological parent wants to place the child up for adoption, while the other does not. If it is contested, a hearing is required in a court of law, and a judge hears evidence and stories from both sides and makes a decision. An adoption attorney is necessary to help make your case and help the judge see your side of the story better.

Freedom from Custody and Control

If a child is to be adopted and it is unknown where one or more of the parents are, it is required that the parental rights are terminated. To do this, you must have a great adoption attorney to help you and to complete the adoption process.
